About US

OUR BEGINNINGS

In 1987, tucked inside a converted 19th-century brick textile mill on the banks of the River Sheger, our family fired up a single antique stone melangeur and began a quiet revolution in Ethiopian chocolate. What started as a weekend hobby—roasting small batches of indigenous white cacao beans that grew wild in the southwestern highlands—quickly attracted the noses and imaginations of neighbors who followed the warm, nutty aroma through the mill’s iron gates. Within a year, the hobby outgrew the kitchen, and the Chocolate Factory was officially born, built on the simple promise that every bar would reflect the land it came from, the hands that tended it, and the community that celebrated it.

Bean-to-Bar Craftsmanship

Today the same mill hums with expanded wings, solar panels, and a zero-waste water system, yet we still hand-sort every sack of beans, stone-grind for 72 hours in small batches, and age our chocolate for a full moon cycle before tempering. Our cacao travels only 480 km from forest to factory—one of the shortest “food miles” in the global chocolate world—allowing us to pay growers above fair-trade premiums while the beans are still warm from the sun. Roasting profiles are tuned to Ethiopia’s unique varietals: bright yellow Amelonado for citrus notes, rare scarlet Criollo for honeyed finish, and a purple-forastero hybrid that lends deep red-fruit undertones. Each recipe is tested, tasted, and voted on by our 42-person worker cooperative (half of whom are women who climbed from pod cleaners to head chocolate makers), ensuring that the chocolate you unwrap carries the soul of the community inside every square.

Sweet Impact & Invitation

Beyond flavor, the factory is a living classroom: local schoolchildren visit to grind their own mini bars, farmers learn post-harvest techniques in our fermentation courtyard, and every October we host the “Cacao Carnival,” where 10 % of annual profits are reinvested into reforesting riverbanks with native shade trees. Our packaging is printed on reclaimed coffee-husk paper, our waste cacao husks fuel the boilers, and the steam that once powered textile looms now warms greenhouse tunnels that grow vanilla and cardamom for next season’s infusions. Step inside any weekday between 9 and 5 and you’ll hear laughter mixing with the slap of cooling chocolate on marble, smell toffee and jasmine in the air, and leave with a square that tells the story of Ethiopian soil, Ethiopian hands, and a tiny riverside factory that believes business can be the most delicious force for good.



WHY YOU SHOULD CHOOSE US

When you bite into a Sheger bar, you’re not just tasting chocolate—you’re tasting integrity. Our beans travel a mere 480 km from forest to factory, the shortest supply chain in Ethiopia, guaranteeing traceability and freshness that mass-market brands simply can’t match. Every grower is paid 30 % above fair-trade minimums within seven days of harvest, a practice that has lifted 1,200 farming families out of subsistence living and into sustainable prosperity. Inside our zero-waste, solar-powered mill, 72-hour stone grinding preserves micronutrients and antioxidants that high-speed industrial mills destroy, giving you a naturally silky mouthfeel without added emulsifiers. Finally, our cooperative ownership model means the same people who roast, wrap, and ship your chocolate also answer the customer-service line—so if you ever have a question, you’re talking directly to the craftsperson whose hands were on your bar.

Order to Delivery

  1. Customer submits order through encrypted website form
  2. Order ticket auto-prints in our fulfillment room within seconds.
  3. Inventory software reserves batch-coded bars from climate-controlled storeroom (18 °C, 55 % RH).
  4. Bars are wrapped in 100 % compostable foil, then cushioned with shredded cacao-husk paper.
  5. Boxes are sealed with water-activated paper tape printed with soy-based ink.
  6. Batch QR code is scanned, linking box to blockchain ledger for farm-to-bite traceability.
  7. Packages are loaded into reusable insulated crates (coconut-fiber liners) and trucked 7 km to Addis Ababa’s central cargo terminal using our electric van.
  8. Crates are transferred to Ethiopian Airlines’ cool-chain facility (15 °C) for same-day domestic flights or two-day international routes
  9. Upon arrival, DHL Express ColdPass picks up and maintains 15–18 °C through last-mile delivery.
  10. Customer receives SMS with real-time temp log and a personal thank-you video.
