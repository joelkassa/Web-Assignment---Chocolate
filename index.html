    Our Beginnings

    In 1987, tucked inside a converted 19th-century brick textile mill on the banks of the River Sheger, our family fired up a single antique stone melangeur and began a quiet revolution in Ethiopian chocolate...

    What started as a weekend hobbyâ€”roasting small batches of indigenous white cacao beansâ€”quickly attracted neighbours who followed the warm aroma through the mill's iron gates.

    Within a year the hobby outgrew the kitchen, and the factory was born, built on the promise that every bar would reflect the land it came from.

    Bean-to-Bar Craftsmanship

    Today the same mill hums with expanded wings, solar panels and a zero-waste water system, yet we still hand-sort every sack of beans, stone-grind for 72 hours in small batches...

    Roasting profiles are tuned to Ethiopia's unique varietals: bright yellow Amelonado for citrus notes, rare scarlet Criollo for honeyed finish, and a purple-forastero hybrid for deep red-fruit undertones.

    Each recipe is tested, tasted and voted on by our 42-person worker cooperative, ensuring that the chocolate you unwrap carries the soul of the community inside every square.

    Sweet Impact

    Beyond flavor, the factory is a living classroom: local schoolchildren visit to grind their own mini bars, farmers learn post-harvest techniques...

    Every October we host the "Cacao Carnival," where 10 % of annual profits are reinvested into reforesting riverbanks with native shade trees.

    Our packaging is printed on reclaimed coffee-husk paper, our waste cacao husks fuel the boilers, and the steam that once powered textile looms now warms greenhouse tunnels.
